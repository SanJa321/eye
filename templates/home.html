Welcome to the Retinal OCT Analysis Platform

Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT) is a powerful imaging technique that provides high-resolution cross-sectional images of the retina, allowing for early detection and monitoring of various retinal diseases. Each year, over 30 million OCT scans are performed, aiding in the diagnosis and management of eye conditions.

Key Features of the Platform
  • Automated Image Analysis: Uses machine learning models to classify OCT images into categories.
  • Cross-Sectional Retinal Imaging: Shows both healthy and diseased retinas for better diagnosis.
  • Streamlined Workflow: Upload, analyze, and review scans efficiently.
Understanding Retinal Diseases through OCT
  1. Choroidal Neovascularization (CNV) - New blood vessel formation beneath the retina.
  2. Diabetic Macular Edema (DME) - Fluid accumulation in the macula due to diabetes.
  3. Drusen (Early AMD) - Yellow deposits under the retina linked to age-related macular degeneration.
  4. Normal Retina - No abnormalities detected.
About the Dataset

Our dataset contains 84,495 high-resolution OCT images (JPEG format) categorized into: Normal, CNV, DME, and Drusen. Each image has been validated for accuracy.
